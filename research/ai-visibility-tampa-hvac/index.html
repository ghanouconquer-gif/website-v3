What Sources ChatGPT Actually Pulled From

ChatGPT did not pull from company websites directly in most cases. It pulled from:

  1. Aggregator "best of" lists (Forbes Home, Angi) — first-layer sources AI reads before recommending
  2. Reddit community threads — peer citations treated as high-trust signals
  3. Google review text — named technicians + specific outcomes = AI-parseable case study
  4. Google Maps structured data — star rating, review count, operating hours, address

A company can be recommended by AI without a great website. A company cannot be recommended without aggregator list presence, high-volume structured reviews, and complete NAP data.

The 6 Citation Signals

Based on analysis across both platforms:

Signal 1 — Review Volume Above Market Threshold

Tampa minimum: 600+ Google reviews for primary ChatGPT citation. Below that = not cited. Above 2,000 = near-guaranteed inclusion. Threshold varies by market — this is Tampa-specific data.

Signal 2 — Named-Technician Review Content

Reviews that name the technician + describe the problem + state the outcome = structured evidence AI extracts. Reviews that say "great service" = zero AI citation value. This is the single most actionable gap across all audited companies.

Signal 3 — 24/7 Availability Declared

Every cited company published 24/7 or emergency availability. This is binary — if AI cannot confirm availability, it will not recommend for emergency queries, which are the highest-converting query category.

Signal 4 — Complete Google Business Profile

Rating, review count, address, hours, service categories, accessibility info, payment methods — all fields populated. Perplexity built its entire recommendation table from GBP data alone. Every blank field = lower structured entity confidence.

Signal 5 — Aggregator List Presence

Forbes, Angi, Expertise.com, BestProsInTown, Thumbtack. These are the sources ChatGPT reads before recommending. A company not on these lists is invisible to ChatGPT regardless of website quality.

Signal 6 — Service-Specific Content on Website

Query intent must match published content language for citation to trigger. Air Rescue was cited for "emergency HVAC" because that language existed in their indexed content. The match is the mechanism.
